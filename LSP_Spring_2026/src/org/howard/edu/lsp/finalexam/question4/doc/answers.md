Over the course of this semester, my perspective on AI in programming has shifted significantly. While I recognize that AI is here to stay, I've become increasingly aware of how over-reliance on it has hindered my actual learning. Because AI allowed me to achieve results with minimal effort, I found myself regressing in my ability to think through problems independently. Much like handing a phone to a child, easy access to powerful AI tools can quietly erode the critical thinking and problem-solving skills that are essential to becoming a competent developer.
The most valuable lesson I took from this experience is that the proper role of AI is as a supplement, not a substitute. Going forward, I plan to first build a genuine understanding of concepts on my own, then use AI to fill gaps or deepen my knowledge in areas where I struggle. I've made a personal commitment to revisit the material from my courses and develop a foundational grasp of everything I previously offloaded to AI. My goal is to treat AI as a companion tool rather than a crutch — something that enhances my abilities rather than replacing them.
